# Zonk

## Rules of the Game

Zonk is played by 2 or more players, in which case the target score is typically 5,000 or 10,000 points. 
If only one player is playing, they strive to score as many points as possible in 10 rounds. 
Often, a certain threshold must be reached (for example, 5,000 points) to achieve victory.

- At the beginning of each round, the player rolls all six dice.
- After each throw, the player must set aside dice in certain scoring combinations.
- If during any throw the remaining dice cannot form a scoring combination, this situation is called a "zonk" and all the points the player earned *during that round* are lost. In some variations, after three zonks in a row, the player loses an additional 500 (or 1,000) points.
- A player can stop their turn (often only if they have scored 300 points or more in a round). Their points are then recorded on the score sheet. The player has the right to continue throwing dice, but over time the likelihood of a zonk (no scoring combinations among the remaining dice) gradually increases.
- If during a round *all 6 dice* have been collected in various combinations, the player earns a bonus throw, which they have the right to decline.
- Once the points for a round have been saved and recorded, the turn is passed to the next player (usually clockwise).
- Once a player has achieved the number of points needed to win, the remaining players are each eligible for one more round to surpass that score. If none of the opponents succeed in doing so, or they waive that right, that player is awarded the win.

## Combinations

Each combination obtained as a result of a throw is worth points.

List of combinations:

### Single Dice

| Combination | Points | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ones | 100 for each | ⚀ | A combination of three ones gives 1,000 points. |
| Fives | 50 for each | ⚄ | A combination of three fives gives 500 points. |

### Three of a Kind

| Combination | Points | Example | 
|---|---|---|---|
| Ones | 1,000 | ⚀⚀⚀ |
| Twos | 200 | ⚁⚁⚁ |
| Threes | 300 | ⚂⚂⚂ |
| Fours | 400 | ⚃⚃⚃ |
| Fives | 500 | ⚄⚄⚄ |
| Sixes | 600 | ⚅⚅⚅ |

### Special Combinations

| Combination | Points | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fourth/fifth/sixth matching die | Each additional die beyond three of a kind scores the same as the original three-of-a-kind value | ⚂⚂⚂ + ⚂ = 300 + 300 | This applies to both the fifth and sixth matching dice. |
| Three pairs | 750 and a bonus throw | ⚀⚀⚁⚁⚄⚄⚅ | If 4 dice show one value and 2 dice show another (for example, ⚁⚁⚁⚁⚄⚄), this 750 combination does not count. Additionally, the three-pair combination can only be obtained in a single throw. |
| Six different values | 1,500 (or 1,000) and a bonus throw | ⚀⚁⚂⚃⚄⚅ | In other rule variations, this amount can be protected from zonks, or 1,500 points are awarded for this combination. |
